Visual Hierarchy

BYU TV

byutv.org

Visual hierarchy refers to the arranging of components in ascending or descending order of importance. This is accomplished using proportion, color, images, contrast, typography, whitespace, texture, and style.

contrast

The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints

churchofjesuschrist.org

Contrast is designing a distinct difference between foreground and background colors in order to enhance readability. Contrast does not only deal with colors but also blends into visual hierarchy of font sizes, image placement, and content.

Hick`s Law

Google

google.com

Hick's Law, also known as the Hick-Hyman Law, states that the time it takes for a person to make a decision increases with the number and complexity of the choices they have. In the context of design, this means that the more options or elements a user has to choose from, the more time it will take for them to make a decision and complete a task.
